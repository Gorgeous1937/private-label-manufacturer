What Is a Private Label Lip Balm Manufacturer?
A private label lip balm manufacturer is a cosmetics production facility that manufactures lip balm formulations - which a brand owner then packages and sells under their own brand name and label. The brand does not own the factory, equipment, or raw material supply chain. This model reduces time-to-market to 30–60 days, compared to 12–18 months for building an in-house manufacturing facility.

For brand owners launching or scaling a lip care line, the private label model offers measurable advantages over in-house manufacturing:

Faster time-to-market: 30–60 days for existing formulations vs 12–18 months for greenfield manufacturing
Lower capex: No investment in plant, machinery, filling lines, or QC laboratory
Pre-tested formulations: Stability-tested, regulatory-compliant formulas available off-the-shelf
Multi-SKU flexibility: Multiple flavours, SPF levels, textures, and formats from a single manufacturer
Certified supply chain: FDA, GMP, and ISO certified production for domestic and export compliance

Kajal Manufacturer in India: Formulations, Regulations & Export
What Is Kajal?
Kajal (also called kohl) is a soft, wax-oil-based eye cosmetic with high pigment concentration, traditionally applied to the inner eyelid rim (waterline) and eyelid. In India, kajal is regulated as a cosmetic under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940. It is a commercially significant product category in South Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa - distinct from eye pencils in formulation, manufacturing process, and regulatory compliance requirements.

India has a strong traditional and commercial heritage in kajal production. Modern Indian kajal manufacturers produce a range of formats suited to both domestic and export B2B buyers:

Regulatory Compliance for Kajal Manufacturing in India
Kajal and eye cosmetics manufactured in India are subject to the following regulatory requirements under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 and rules thereunder:

No lead-based ingredients: Commercial kajal must not contain lead compounds. Lead-containing kohl (surma) is prohibited for commercial cosmetics under CDSCO guidelines.
Heavy metal limits: Arsenic, lead, and mercury content must comply with BIS standards (IS 4011 and IS 4707 cover eye cosmetics). As per Bureau of Indian Standards norms, lead should not exceed 20 ppm in cosmetics intended for eye area use.
Approved colorants only: Colorants must be from the approved list under Schedule Q of the Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945.
Cosmetics Manufacturing Licence: The facility must hold a valid licence in Form COS-2 issued by the State Licensing Authority under Schedule M.
Sources: CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organisation); Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940; Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS)

Kajal Export Compliance: GCC & European Markets
Kajal exported from India to GCC countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Qatar) must comply with GSO 1943 (Gulf Standards Organization) cosmetics regulations. Lead limits are typically stricter in GCC than domestic Indian standards. For European Union export, kajal must comply with EC Regulation 1223/2009, including approved colorant lists under Annex IV and prohibited substances under Annex II. A Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR) prepared by a qualified EU safety assessor is mandatory.

How Private Label Color Cosmetics Manufacturing Works (Step by Step)
The standard process for ordering private label lip balm, kajal, lipstick, or any color cosmetic from a certified Indian manufacturer follows these six stages:

Initial Enquiry & Brief
Share product category, format, target market, quantity, regulatory requirements, and budget range. Typical duration: 1–3 days for quotation.

Formulation Selection / R&D
Choose from existing stable formulations or initiate custom formulation with the manufacturer's R&D team. Custom R&D: 2–6 weeks additional.

Lab Sample Evaluation
Receive physical lab samples. Multiple revision rounds are standard before final formulation approval. Typical duration: 1–3 weeks.

Stability Testing
Accelerated stability testing under Schedule M requirements ensures shelf life and export compliance. Accelerated testing: 4–12 weeks.

Packaging & Artwork Finalisation
Finalise primary and secondary packaging, label artwork (as per brand guidelines), and regulatory labelling requirements for target market.

Bulk Production & Delivery
Full production run with in-process QC, finished product testing, and delivery on agreed timelines with complete documentation. Typical bulk production: 15–30 working days.
